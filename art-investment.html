Investment Strategy

Art as Investment

Understanding art as both cultural and financial asset—balancing aesthetic value with long-term appreciation potential.

Research & Due Diligence

Successful art investment requires rigorous analysis across multiple dimensions. CADA evaluates acquisition opportunities based on three core considerations:

Artistic Merit

Does the work demonstrate exceptional quality, innovation, and cultural significance? Is the artist contributing to important conversations within contemporary art?

Market Position

What is the artist's institutional recognition? What galleries represent them? Which museums have acquired their work? What is their auction track record?

Artist Trajectory

Is the artist early-career, mid-career, or established? What is their production rate? How has their work evolved? What is the strength of their critical reception?

Key Term

Provenance

Provenance refers to the documented history of an artwork's ownership. Strong provenance—particularly when it includes reputable galleries, collectors, or institutions—enhances both the authenticity and market value of a work.

Risk & Portfolio Considerations

Art is an alternative asset class with unique characteristics that distinguish it from stocks, bonds, and real estate. Collectors should be aware of several key risk factors:

Illiquidity

Art cannot be quickly converted to cash without potential loss of value. Investment horizons should typically be 7-10 years or longer.

Market Volatility

Art markets can be cyclical and influenced by broader economic trends, collector taste shifts, and institutional priorities.

Authentication

Questions of authenticity, attribution, and condition can significantly impact value. Proper documentation is essential.

Transaction Costs

Auction premiums, gallery commissions, insurance, storage, and conservation can represent significant ongoing costs.

Time Horizons & Appreciation

Historical data shows that top-tier contemporary art has appreciated at rates comparable to or exceeding traditional asset classes over long time periods. However, this appreciation is not evenly distributed—it is concentrated among artists with strong institutional support, critical recognition, and sustained market demand.

Contemporary African Diaspora Art represents one of the fastest-growing segments of the global art market, driven by increasing institutional recognition, demographic shifts in collector bases, and long-overdue corrections to historical exclusions from the canon.

Emerging Markets

CADA has particular expertise in identifying emerging artists and markets with strong appreciation potential. Key indicators include:

  • → Museum acquisitions and institutional exhibitions
  • → Representation by top-tier international galleries
  • → Critical attention from major publications and curators
  • → Participation in significant biennials and art fairs
  • → Strong auction results with sustained upward trajectories
